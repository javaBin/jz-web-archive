Organizer

The organizer of JavaZone is javaBin, an independent, non-profit organization. It’s objective is to promote Java expertise among its members, their companies and in the market. javaBin is based in Oslo, and there are also groups in Sørlandet, Stavanger, Bergen, Trondheim and Innlandet.

javaBin currently has more than 2,000 members from a wide range of companies.

javaBin organize monthly meetings for it’s members. The meetings consist of presentations from members, members’ companies or external speakers. The meetings bring together experts representing wide-ranging software development know-how.

These meetings are a good way to build the java platform’s market, and provide an arena for exchanging experiences and information; they also have a social side, promoting contact across companies and professional environments.

The Java user group in Norway was first set up in the summer of 1996. It faded out shortly afterwards, until a number of prime movers took the initiative to hold a founders meeting on 23 April 1998.

Volunteers

The success of the event depends on the help of volunteers and we are looking for conference assistants. Volunteers are typically IT students or others with an interest in Java who want to help with the practicalities of the conference, such as rigging, filming and so forth.

In exchange for a few hours’ effort during the conference, you will get free entrance, tons of technical input from the industry, access to many potential employers, a valuable reference on your CV and other fringe benefits. Plus, we will also be hosting a volunteers’ party at Blindern/UiO after the conference!
